Free Response Questions (45 points total)

FRQ 1: Work-Energy Problem (15 points)

Two identical blocks are released from rest at the top of identical ramps, each 5 meters long and inclined at 30° to the horizontal. Ramp A is frictionless while Ramp B has a coefficient of kinetic friction μk = 0.25. Both blocks slide into a horizontal surface - Ramp A's surface is frictionless while Ramp B's has the same coefficient of friction as its ramp. The blocks come to rest at points A and B respectively. The blocks are 1kg each.
Diagram of setup

a) Calculate the speed of each block at the bottom of its ramp. (6 points)
b) Calculate the horizontal distance traveled by block A after leaving the ramp. (4 points)
c) Calculate the horizontal distance traveled by block B after leaving the ramp. (3 points)
d) Explain why block A travels further in terms of energy conservation and work done by friction. (2 points)

FRQ 2: Spring Energy Problem (12 points)

A spring with spring constant k = 200 N/m is mounted horizontally on a frictionless table. The spring is compressed 0.15 m and used to launch a 0.5 kg block.
Diagram of the system over time

a) Calculate the energy stored in the compressed spring. (2 points)
b) If the spring launches the block up a frictionless ramp inclined at 25° to the horizontal, what maximum height will the block reach? (4 points)
c) If instead the spring launches the block horizontally along a surface with coefficient of kinetic friction μk = 0.2, how far will the block slide before stopping? (4 points)
d) Compare the distances in parts (b) and (c). Why are they different? (2 points)

FRQ 3: Energy Analysis Problem (10 points)

A pendulum consists of a 0.2 kg ball attached to a 1.0 m long string. The pendulum is released from rest when the string makes a 30° angle with the vertical. A light sensor measures the ball's speed at the bottom of its swing.
NOTE: This problem takes advantage of trigonometry. Remember, SOH-CAH-TOA
Diagram showing the pendulum at our release point, and the lowest point

a) On the diagram below, draw arrows showing the direction and relative magnitude of the gravitational potential energy and kinetic energy at:

  • The release point
  • The lowest point
  • A point halfway between release and lowest point
    (3 points)

b) Calculate the ball's speed at the lowest point of its swing. (3 points)

c) The actual measured speed is slightly less than your calculated value. Explain this difference and identify what form of energy was not considered in your calculation. (2 points)

d) If the string length is doubled but the release angle stays the same, explain how this affects:

  • The maximum potential energy

FRQ 4: Experimental Design Problem (8 points)

A student wants to determine the spring constant of a spring by measuring energy conversions instead of using Hooke's Law directly.

a) Design an experiment using the following equipment:

  • A spring
  • A set of masses
  • A meterstick
  • A table with negligible friction
    Describe your procedure and what measurements you would take. (3 points)

b) Explain how you would use your measurements to calculate the spring constant. Include:

  • The equations you would use
  • How you would graph your data
  • What the slope of your graph would represent
    (3 points)

c) Identify two sources of uncertainty in your measurements and explain how each would affect your calculated spring constant. (2 points)
