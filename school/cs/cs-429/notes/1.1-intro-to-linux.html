If I have infinite memory, can't I make the entire search space and output space cached? Then I get runtime from accessing the hashtable

To have the illusion of infinity in speed or space, the tradeoffs are the cruz of comp-arch

The hierarchy of cache has to do with speed of access and frequency. You can get a high speed of access trading off for the amount of storage. A fridge is an L1 cache. The grocery store is an L2 cache. The actual farm might be the L3 cache. If I need a huge load of memory usage at once, I might need to go to L3 and it'll take awhile. But if I just need to get a bit of milk, I can go straight to the fridge and it'll be really fast

The cache is the one nearest to the processor.

ls -lt lists the subdirectories in order of time
ls -ltr lists the subdirectories in reverse order of time

Controlling access on shared machines

Doign ls -l, the first column tells you the permissions available to the other users on the comuter.
The first 3 chars are for me. The second 3 are for the group. The last 3 are for the world

Letter Meaning
r read
w write
x execute

These are octal numbers! Having any of these charecteristics is just a 1 or a 0.
