Edge Triggered D Flip Flop

We want to make it so our reactions re dependent on the edges of a signal. To do that, we have two latches, each in the opposite state of the other.

It's like an airlock. When the door to the airlock is open, anyone can go in but no one can leave the ship. Then when we open the vent to space, the front door closes and the outside opens. The only difference is that the only signal sent across is the last input signal sent in as opposed to a queue.

It makes sense how these circuits behave with a binary signal, but what happens to an analog signal? What if I never flattened my sin wave from the LC generator circuit? Does this then take a derivative?

In our model we're working with completely ideal circuits. In reality we would have all our signals be continuous, but we're idealized.
That doesn't answer my question though, which is what does it look like when I send an analog signal into my Edge Triggered D Flip Flop
