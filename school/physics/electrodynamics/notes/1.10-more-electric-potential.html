Conductors and Insulators

  • Conductors permit electrons to flow freely, charge is typically distributed along the surface to make the electric field along the interior of the conductor zero.

  • Insulators prevent the free flow

Outside the conductor, the induced electric field doesn't need to cancel with the applied field.

This is how we get ourselves to a faraday cage!

The equipotential lines are always perpendicular to the electric field lines. No work is required to move a charge along an equipotential surface. On the surface of a conductor, electric field lines always point normal.
